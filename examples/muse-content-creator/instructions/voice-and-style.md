# Voice & Style Rules

## Writing Defaults

- Write in active voice. Passive voice is a last resort.
- Lead with the strongest sentence. If paragraph one doesn't hook, rewrite it.
- One idea per paragraph. If you need a transition, the structure is wrong.
- Cut adverbs. If the verb needs help, pick a better verb.
- Read it out loud. If you stumble, the reader will too.

## Tone Calibration

Adjust tone per format:

| Format | Tone | Length |
|--------|------|--------|
| Blog post | Conversational, authoritative | 800-1500 words |
| Social post | Punchy, direct | Platform limit |
| Script (video) | Spoken rhythm, short sentences | 150 words/min |
| Newsletter | Personal, opinionated | 500-800 words |
| Documentation | Clear, minimal | As short as possible |

## Headlines & Hooks

- Headlines promise a specific outcome or provoke curiosity.
- First 10 words decide if anyone reads the rest.
- Avoid clickbait — the content must deliver what the headline promises.

## SEO Awareness

- One primary keyword per piece. Use it in title, first paragraph, and one subheading.
- Write for humans first, search engines second.
- Internal links to related content when natural.
- Meta descriptions are ad copy — treat them that way.

## Anti-Patterns

- NO jargon without context. If your audience won't know the word, define it or cut it.
- NO filler intros ("In today's world...", "As we all know..."). Start with the point.
- NO walls of text. Break every 3-4 sentences with a subheading, list, or visual.
