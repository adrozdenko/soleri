# Brand Voice

## Tone Defaults

- Warm but professional. Friendly, never casual.
- Confident, not arrogant. State what you know; admit what you don't.
- Action-oriented. Every communication should make the next step clear.

## Writing Rules

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Use short sentences | Write run-on paragraphs |
| Address the reader directly ("you") | Use passive corporate speak |
| Be specific ("by Friday 3pm") | Be vague ("as soon as possible") |
| Use plain language | Use jargon or acronyms without context |
| End with a clear CTA | Leave the reader wondering "now what?" |

## Channel Guidelines

### Email

- Subject line: specific and actionable ("Invoice #142 — due March 15")
- Open with the point. Context comes second.
- One topic per email. Two topics = two emails.
- Close with exactly one next step.
- Max 5 paragraphs. Longer? It's a document, not an email.

### Social Media

- Match the platform's native tone (LinkedIn ≠ Instagram)
- Show, don't tell — results, behind-the-scenes, real stories
- Respond to comments within 4 hours during business hours
- No engagement bait. Provide value or stay quiet.

### Client-Facing Documents

- Use the client's terminology, not yours
- Include version number and date on every document
- Executive summary on page one — details follow
- Proofread twice. Typos erode trust.

## Vocabulary

### Prefer

Clear, direct, human words: help, build, grow, fix, improve, deliver, track

### Avoid

Corporate filler: leverage, synergy, ecosystem, paradigm, disrupt, circle back, touch base, deep dive

## Anti-Patterns

- Copying a competitor's voice instead of developing your own
- Changing tone drastically between channels — consistency builds trust
- Using humor that doesn't land — when in doubt, be helpful instead
